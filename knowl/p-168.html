We'll focus on how to solve inequalities by applying at each step a (one-to-one) function to each side. For example, we want to think of the step

\begin{align*} 19x-12 &\lt 45\\ \color{cyan}{+12}& \;\; \color{cyan}{+12} && \text{"add 12 to both sides"}\\ 19x &\lt 57 \end{align*}

as, instead, the application of the function \(f(x)=x+12\) to both sides:

\begin{align*} 19x-12 &\lt 45\\ \color{cyan}{f({}}19x-12\color{cyan}{{})} & \;\; \color{cyan}{f({}} 45 \color{cyan}{{})} && \text{"Apply }f(x)=x+12\text{ to both sides"}\\ 19x &\lt 57. \end{align*}
