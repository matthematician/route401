Definition A.2. Parity, Even, Odd.

Let \(n\in \mathbb{Z}\) be an integer. We say that the parity of \(n\) is either even or odd depending on whether:

\begin{align} \text{There exists }k\in\mathbb{Z}\text{ such that } && n &= 2k & \text{(}n\text{ is even)}\tag{A.1}\\ \text{There exists }k\in\mathbb{Z}\text{ such that } && n &= 2k+1 & \text{(}n\text{ is odd)}\tag{A.2} \end{align}
